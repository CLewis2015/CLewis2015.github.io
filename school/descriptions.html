Instructional Technology Tools

Designed for both mainstream teachers and ESOL teachers, this session will prepare you to differentiate instruction using a variety of cutting-edge tech tools. You will develop technological expertise that returns you to the classroom better prepared to meet the various learning needs and styles of your students.

New Science Standards

This session will review the South Carolina Academic Standards and Performance Indicators for Science. We will go over the academic standards in science for students in sixth through twelfth grade.

Science Safety

Basic review of lab safety. This includes labeling, storing, handling and disposing of chemicals, filling out your science-safety checklist, guidelines for student accidents, precautions for animals and plants in the lab, protective equipment, and sterilization.

CCSS Science Writing

Develop the skills necessary to teach students how to read and write scientific papers while meeting common core standards.

SmartBoard Use and Tools

SmartBoard session ranging from basic SmartBoard instruction for beginners to using advanced features of the Smart Notebook software and learning how to incorporate them into daily lessons.

Classroom First Aid

Basic first aid procedures and how to handle accidents and emergencies in the classroom.

Using Discus Resources in the Classroom

Learn to use and incorporate South Carolina's Virtual Library into your lesson plans and research projects.

Powerful Presentations

Turn your basic PowerPoint Presentation into an engaging lecture that will keep your students' attention. We will cover color themes, student interaction, animations, and sound effects.

Teacher Toolbox

Teacher Toolbox is full of useful, easy to access resources for all teachers. Come hear about all the resources and how to launch your CFAs!!

Gang Awareness

This session will include information on signs, symbols, graffiti, tattoos, colors, structure, and current events with criminal street gangs in South Carolina. It will help you become aware of students who are in gangs or who want to be and how to help them make better choices.

Research Remix

Research Remix is a combination of strategies, graphic organizers, and activities to help students with research skills. Examples will focus on the science standards.

Data and More Data

I have soooo much data! Which data should I use when? How do I connect student data to instruction? This session will help you understand your data.
Required Materials: Copy of student MAP data and student CFA summary data.
